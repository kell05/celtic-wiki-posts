Season Review 2017-18

Matches: 2017 - 2018 | 2017-18 Pictures | League Table | Statistics

Trivia

  • Celtic win the Treble (Scottish Premiership, Scottish Cup, Scottish League Cup)

  • Celtic make history by being the first team in top flight history (probably globally) to have done this back-to-back.
  • Celtic have now won six domestic trophies in a row, Brendan Rodgers has won 8/8 games at Hampden (4 finals plus other cup games).
  • Celtic have achieved >1,000,000 total home attendance in all matches this season. Seventh highest support in Europe, higher than any club in Serie A. List of clubs who haven't include: Milan, Juventus, Liverpool, Schalke, PSG, Chelsea, Atlético Madrid, Man City & Sevco.
  • Average attendances at the six clubs who finished in the top half of the SPFL Premiership in the 2017/18 season are up across the board. Hibs at a 50 year high, excellent news for the game.
  • Celtic were unbeaten Invincibles last season domestically, question was if they repeat the incredible feat this season was the big question. Sadly no for Invincibles.
  • Brendan Rodgers second season in charge.
  • Celtic have now won six domestic trophies in a row. Seven is the record in Scotland.
  • Handful of league defeats, and plenty of draws but still clearly won the league.
  • In Scottish Cup final, only two players were Rodgers transfers. Questions being raised over his transfer record, some great successes but plenty of failures.
  • Musonda was big name loan player but lasted six months before being papped out.
  • Number of injuries was frustrating: Roberts, Dembele, Edouard, Gordon etc.
  • Record high attendances in Scottish Premiership across many clubs which was great to see.
  • Scott Brown was cleaning up player of the year awards, and wiping the floor with Hearts manager Craig Levein who said players needed protected from his play.
  • Tierney continues to excel, but was a repeat target for lumberjacks in the game who mercilessly hacked him.
  • Celtic qualified for Champions League after three rounds, including contentious matches v Linfield.
  • Celtic suffered worst home defeat for over 100 years, 5-0 loss to PSG, then 7-0 away to PSG.
  • Celtic recorded a rare away win in the Champions League Group stages, 3-0 v Anderlecht.
  • Scotland fail to make the world cup coming third in their group under Gordon Strachan who lost his role, but Martin O'Neill with Roy Keane kept his role at Ireland despite not making the World Cup either.
  • FIFA award Celtic the Best fans in their world award in their annual event.
  • Celtic qualified for Europa League KO stage following third place finish, but ko'ed in first KO round.
  • Brendan Rodgers has defeated 3x Sevco managers by at least 4 goals in the past 12 months.
  • Brendan Rodgers has now won an incredible 8 matches without defeat at Hampden.
  • Brendan Rodgers Celtic has scored 30 goals v Sevco in two seasons.
  • Celtic undefeated run ends after 69 games with a crash (4-0 defeat to Hearts).
  • Hibs are resurgent under ex-Celt Neil Lennon, and Aberdeen are doing well too.
  • Sevco lose two managers this season (hapless Caixhina and out of his depth Murty), then appoint Liverpool playing legend Stevie Gerrard who is a rookie manager and previously played under Rodgers.
  • Hibs manage to remain undefeated at home v Celtic, quite a feat.


Season Review

Start of the season (June 2017):
 SFA President Alan McRae: “It was a fantastic achievement [for Celtic] to come through both cups and the league undefeated – it was amazing. But I hope it won’t happen again next season because we’ve got to have challengers. They’ll know you need to have other people challenging otherwise it will get boring and dreary. We definitely need a good, strong Rangers."
