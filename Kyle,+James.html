Biog

Signed from Benburb in 1890 left-half James Kyle made his debut in a 5-0 league win at Cowlairs on 29th April 1891.

He was the captain of Benburb (a team based in Govan) and one of Celtic's many captures from "The Polmadie Greens" which included Johnny Campbell, Barney Crossan, Joe Cullen and Johnny Cunningham.

He had the curious nickname of "Fish"!

Some records seem to state he played in the 1-1 draw v Rangers on 7th May 1892, but others don't (for example '125 years of Celts' by Potter & Rowan). Records weren't as well kept then as now. We have credited him an appearance in the table below, that match at least is something he later likely cherished (assuming he did play in it). That match was the first time that goal nets were used at Celtic Park. On an aside, Major Burke of the travelling Buffalo Bills Wild West Show from the USA kicked off the match.

He was to only make one more competitive appearance for the Bhoys, a long wait until the following season when he deputised for Peter Dowd in a 9-2 win over Linthouse in the Glasgow Charity Cup semi-final.

Celtic finished the 1891-92 season as runners-up by just 2pts behind Dumbarton, who finished the season with an exceptional home record, winning all eleven home matches. Celtic were not far off with ten home wins and one home draw. A tough and competitive league season, with Hearts only 1pt further behind Celtic.

He was released in 1892.
