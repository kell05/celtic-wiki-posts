Review

(Tim Waits of KDS)
Utterly dominant and we played far better than the scoreline suggests. Well done Stokes, especially after being much maligned (including by me), because a draw wasn't an option and he stepped up and did the necessary. Can't believe our best new signing only lasted about half an hour ... really hoping he's back in time for Wednesday.

Middle to front the team looks good other than the much discussed lack of goals ... thought Brown and Ledley were excellent in midfield, the attacking play was good enough to create half a dozen clear chances.

The fullbacks all had good games I thought ... Efe, though, oh dear ... though he seemed to get a bit more composure as the game went on.

Thank ghod the football's back anyway ...

Celtic 2 Ross County 1: Stokes steps into the breach

Stewart Fisher
Sports Writer, The Herald
Sunday 4 August 2013

JOHN Keane, the man who stepped in at the death with a £1 million bond to keep Celtic out of administration back in 1994, was handed the honour of unfurling the championship flag at Parkhead yesterday.

Little did the fans suspect it would take another 11th-hour intervention to make sure the club picked up all three points. Only three minutes remained when Anthony Stokes shifted the ball away from Brian McLean and on to his right foot to fire in the goal which allowed Neil Lennon's side to begin their pursuit of a maiden SPFL Premiership title with a win.

The goal was late but hardly undeserved. Although Parkhead was stunned into silence by a blistering strike from Ross County's young Dutchman Darren Maatsen after just three minutes, the general narrative of this match was Celtic's post-Gary Hooper inability to convert the chances they created.

Lennon afterwards pronounced himself pleased with a patient performance, and praised the contribution of Stokes, who had also bagged the equaliser, even if his pursuit of a further striker - perhaps Kevin Doyle - remains a priority. "We worked the goalkeeper very well, hit the post and had umpteen crosses flash across goal," the Celtic manager said. "The build-up play was very pleasing and that is something we haven't had for a while.

"Anthony will maybe not get the volume of goals Gary would get but if you look at his record it is maybe one in two so he is one we really don't want to go. I just want him to keep playing the way he has played the last three years."

The two teams shared one win apiece and two draws last season but the Ross County side which afforded Celtic a guard of honour was unrecognisable from that which took the scalp of Lennon's side in Dingwall in April.

The Victoria Park club have put their faith this summer in a job lot of young Dutchmen and it didn't take long for one of them to make an impact.

The 22-year-old midfielder picked up a square pass from Stuart Kettlewell on the edge of the box, and unleashed a fierce drive which flew past Fraser Forster and hit the underside of the bar twice before ending up in the Englishman's net. "I've never hit a ball like that before," he admitted afterwards.

The hush which fell over Celtic Park after that goal was replicated just after the half-hour mark when the debut of another Dutchman, Derk Boerrigter, was brought to a premature end.

Celtic's new winger had lived up to his billing during his half-hour on the field - a good athlete who was inches away from getting on the end of at least three crosses - but did little to dispel a reputation for being injury-prone when he crumpled to the turf with what looked like an ankle problem.

In addition to Boerrigter's efforts, James Forrest and Kris Commons - who forced County goalkeeper Mark Brown into a smart stop from 10 yards - also went close before the opener arrived. Graham Carey's poor touch led to a goalmouth scramble, Mikael Lustig possibly using an arm before turning a Joe Ledley shot towards goal and the ball trundling off a post before Stokes knocked it over the line from inches out.

The phrase one-way traffic was a fair assessment of the second half, even if County's five-man backline defended resolutely. Stokes was off target from another Forrest cross, Commons rattled the bar after a good cross from substitute Tony Watt, Charlie Mulgrew struck a post with a header from a Commons corner, and there were countless last-ditch blocks besides.

Parkhead howled its displeasure at Willie Collum's non-award of a penalty when Commons and went to ground with Brian McLean in close attendance - Lennon was spoken to by the official for venting his feelings about the incident - before the late goal made the debate rather irrelevant. "I would have taken a 2-1 defeat before the start," County manager Derek Adams said. "From my point of view our season starts next week."


BBC
By Kenny Crawford BBC Sport Scotland
Comments (211)

Reigning champions Celtic came back from behind to beat Ross County in their Scottish Premiership opener.

County's Darren Maatsen shocked Celtic Park with an emphatic early goal from distance.

Hoops' Dutch debutant Derk Boerrigter looked lively before Anthony Stokes levelled from a yard out for the hosts.

Celtic hit the woodwork twice after the break and Stokes was denied a penalty before the striker's late near-post finish clinched three points.

It was so nearly an early dent to the 90-point-total Celtic manager Neil Lennon has set for 2013-14 in the inaugural year of the Scottish Professional Football League's Premiership.

Last season, a tally of 79 points was amassed as the final Scottish Premier League title was secured by Lennon's side, and the champions' flag was unfurled before kick-off.

Eyes focused largely on Dutch debutants with former Ajax winger Boerrigter in the hosts' line-up and Ross County fielding Maatsen and forward Kevin Luckassen.
Derk Boerrigter

Celtic's Derk Boerrigter made a lively debut but was forced off with an injury after half an hour

County were beginning only their second season in Scotland's top flight and a fifth-place finish last time round means hopes are high for the Dingwall side during the new campaign.

Supporters' minds have still to be made up on the reliability of Hoops defender Efe Ambrose and when Ross County's Graham Carey charged down the Nigerian's first minute clearance, hearts were in mouths again.

Early Celtic shakiness gave way to sheer County brilliance when one of their five new players, Maatsen, thumped home a brilliant opening goal off the underside of the crossbar.

Neat play by Carey set up the Dutchman's right-footed smash from the corner of the penalty box.

Boerrigter did his best to give Celtic fans a positive first impression, one header wide and a left-foot shot well saved by County's Mark Brown punctuating a generally lively offering.

Visiting goalkeeper Brown earned further plaudits by beating away Commons' fierce drive but his goal was about to be breached in scrappy style.

Amid shouts for offside, it was Celtic striker Stokes who turned in from a yard after Ledley's effort had been blocked and then Boeriggter's follow-up rebounded off a post.
Analysis
Ross County celebrate Darren Maatsen's third-minute goal

"Ross County did what Ross County do. They work hard and they're organised; that's why they'll get good results throughout the season." BBC Radio Scotland pundit Billy Dodds

The Highlanders rarely got out of their own half and what was almost Boeriggter's debut goal became his last contribution when he slid for a cross and fell awkwardly near defender Mihael Kovacevic.

Stokes has shown reluctance to sign a new Celtic contract but was much sprightlier in hitting a snap-shot narrowly wide after the re-start.

Commons continued the one-way traffic by hitting the crossbar and then the midriff of a sprawling Branislav Micic within a four-minute period.

Woodwork was struck again when Ledley's header hit the post as goalkeeper Brown helplessly looked on.

County's rearguard weathered relentless Celtic attacks but when their new defender Brian McLean appeared to push Stokes in the box the whole stadium awaited referee Willie Collum's whistle, which never came.

But Irishman Stokes continued his telepathic passing understanding with Commons until the latter stages, and benefitted from his cute pass to fire in at Brown's near post.
