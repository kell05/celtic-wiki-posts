Biog

Ewan was a product of the Celtic youth system (Celtic academy graduate), and is the younger brother of former Celtic prospect Liam Henderson who was close but unfortunate not to make a long term career at Celtic (but still highly impressed). Ewan's challenge was to see if he could succeed where Liam didn't at Celtic, however Liam Henderson had now made a name for himself by winning a deserved moved to Bari in Italy after some good spells in between at various other clubs.

Ewan was to make his debut at a dead rubber end of the season match, in the penultimate league game v Kilmarnock with Celtic heading for the second domestic treble in a row. In a makeshift side, Ewan was subbed late on, but Celtic were abject throughout as they drew nil-nil with only a single goal on target from both sides (Kilmarnock didn't even have a shot at goal). For Ewan a memorable moment as reports gave him positive marks for his brief debut, but for the rest of the support it was a very forgettable game.

Understandably, as he was given little game time since his debut there were reports of unrest from the player in hope to get experience elsewhere. However, the club had faith in him and he was to sign a further long-term contract with the club in January 2019, but was soon loaned out in the summer to help get him First Team gametime at a club.
