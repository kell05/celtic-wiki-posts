Trivia

Much had happened at Celtic during the closed period between the end of the 74-75 season and this the start of the 75-76 season

  • At some point after the end of the previous season Kenny Dalglish had submitted a transfer request. This was kept very quiet and not picked up by the press but there were rumours that Leeds Utd had made a £240,000 bid. With Jock Stein's crash everything went on hold and in fact Dalglish signed a new 2 year contract.
  • Jimmy Johnstone and Jim Brogan had both been freed in June with Johnstone signed a 1 year deal with Californian side San Jose Earthquakes on the 18th June and Jim Brogan would join Coventry City in August
  • Dalglish and Danny McGrain were busy with the Scotland senior side in the Home internationals as well as against Romania. Pat McCluskey played for the Scotland U-23 side.
  • In July Celtic Chairman Desmond White had talked of how the club would revert to a 'youth' policy it had followed in the past which brought the Lisbon Lions together though they would move into the transfer market if the right player was available at the right price.
  • On the 5th of July at 3:00am in the morning Jock Stein was seriously injured in a crash on the A74 near Lockerbie when the car he was driving after arriving back from holiday in Minorca was involved in an accident. The Celtic manager received serious head and chest injuries that would see him sidelined for the season with Sean Fallon taking over the running of the club.
  • Peter Latchford had been signed as a full player after playing the latter part of the last season as a loanee from West Bromwich Albion. Celtic paid £30,000 for him on the 14th July.
  • Graham Barclay moved to Dunfermiline on loan.

  • The players had reported back for pre-season training on the 14th July. This included George Connelly who had ended the previous season walking out again. Steve Murray would start the season out injured and Brian McLaughlin was allowed extra time out to sort out domestic issues. Celtic flew to Ireland with a 17-man squad on the 21st July leaving George Connelly and Dixie Deans back in Glasgow. Bobby Lennox would captain the side for the three match tour.
  • Celtic were joined by trialist Johannes Edvaldsson. An Icelandic international Edvaldsson had been playing in Denmark and had just finished in the Danish side Holbeck's season. He was due back in Iceland to captain the national side. Normally a centre half he could also play in midfield. He was studying physiothrapy part time.
  • Played at Oriel Park, in a carnival atmosphere. The Celtic party were based at Ballybofey.
  • Edvaldsson impresses in his first game with good passing and ball work.
  • Middlesborough's goalkeeper Jim Platt guests for Dundalk.
  • Dalglish, who has asked for a transfer, scores one of his greatest ever goals a perfect shot into the postage stamp corner.
  • After the game whilst the rest of the players were being entertained at a buffet Harry Hood had his arm X-Rayed at a local hospital. Bad bruising was the outcome and Harry went off to the golf course.
  • Dundalk's centre forward was Jim 'Chang' Smith who would play for each of the teams against Celtic on this three match tour and was later invited to a trial at Celtic Park in early September.

Review

Hood, Glavin and Edvaldsson lined up in midfield.
Harry Hood scored direct from a free kick. Dalglish, Ronnie Galvin and Danny McGrain combined to give Glavin the second goal. Dalglish hit a perfect chipped shot for the third.
In the second half Bobby Lennox got the fifth after a move involving Wilson and Dalglish. Harry Hood went down with a shoulder injury on 59 minutes and Sean Fallon took the opportunity to sub Lennox, Glavin and Hood. Paul Wilson kicked in a sixth just before the final whistle.
