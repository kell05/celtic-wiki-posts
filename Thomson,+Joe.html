Biog

A product of the youth academy, Joe Thomson was a promising midfielder who had won praise in the youth ranks, and impressed Celtic staff to sign on for a two year contract.

Thomson was born in Paisley and played for Gleniffer Thistle Boys Club before joining the Celtic Development squad in 2013. He signed a two-year contract with the Hoops in June this year.The 19-year-old is highly rated by Celtic and was a key part of the Development Squad that finished the SPFL Development League as undefeated title-winners last season. Described as a box-to-box midfielder, Thomson has been capped at all levels up to Under-19 level for Scotland.

He was said to be able to play across the midfield, with his attacking instincts bringing him a regular goal return, including strikes in the UEFA Youth League.

He won a deserved place for the first team, in a friendly v Villareal in 2015, but his first senior competitive outing was at the start of the 2015-16 season with Celtic running out 3-1 victors over Dundee Utd in the league, just as Celtic were revving up for the start of the season.

He would still go on to score his first senior goal for the club during a mid-season friendly in the friendly Maspalomas International Cup, when, having come on as a substitute, he struck against Sparta Prague.

However, after the previously mentioned Dundee Utd match, the wheels came off at Celtic, crashing out of the European Cup to Malmo, and Ronnie Deila's reign was beginning to fall part. This wasn't going to help young players like Joe Thomson. As Celtic's season began to falter, with genuine fears at one point that Aberdeen could topple Celtic to the title, youngsters were put in the shade. Ronnie Deila had actually an excellent record with young players in giving them a chance, and Thomson was named in the squads to face both Kilmarnock and Hamilton in the league but no more senior competitive games.

He was working making the effort:
“I just need to progress as a player and if I can work on the aspects that I know I need to improve on then hopefully I can make a real push. If not, maybe I need to go out and play some form of first-team football but that’s hopefully going to be here.”

He was loaned out to Dumbarton, Livingston and Queen of the South for experience. With Brendan Rodgers arriving at Celtic with (initially) less of an emphasis on playing youth and an incredibly over packed squad with midfielders, it wasn't going to be easy for anyone. Celtic were on their way to back-to-back trebles, so the writing was on the wall for some of the youngsters back in the queue. There was actually a rich vein of young blood coming through at Celtic but not all could be accommodated, and some sadly had to be moved on for their own benefit.

On 16 June 2018, Thomson was freed by Celtic but was then signed for second tier side Dunfermline Athletic. Thomson was voted fans player of the year in his first season with Dunfermline Athletic.

Away from football Thomson is said to hold a black belt in freestyle kickboxing.

We wish him the best.

Joe Thomson commits future to Celtic

By: Martin Dalziel in Slovenia on 29 Jun, 2016 17:03

  • http://www.celticfc.net/news/10789
    CELTIC is delighted to announce that Joe Thomson has signed a two year contract with the club, keeping him at the Scottish champions until 2018.The young Hoops midfielder made his competitive debut last season in the second-half of a 3-1 victory over Dundee United at Tannadice and had previously featured for the first-team in a string of friendlies over the course of the past two seasons.The 19-year-old is highly rated by the club and was a key part of the Development Squad that finished the SPFL Development League as undefeated title-winners last season.Thomson started pre-season this year training with Brendan Rodgers’ first-team at Lennoxtown and told the official Celtic website in an exclusive interview that he was delighted to commit his immediate future to the Bhoys.“I was pleased to get offered the deal and I have to now keep my feet on the ground, keep focused and not get ahead of myself then just show people why I’ve been offered the deal and then kick-on,” he said.“First and foremost I need to try to get the manager’s eye in training and take it from there. It remains to be seen if I need to go on loan or not to do that but I’ll just give 100 per cent in every session and game.“I’ve trained with the first-team a couple of times before they went away for pre-season and the training seems very good. It’s a high intensity and everyone is working hard.“Although I only played 15 minutes on my debut last season I learned a lot from it. It was a lot quicker than it was at Under-20s and even in the pre-season friendlies it was still a lot faster and a jump up but that’s one I want and need to make if I want to get anywhere myself.”Thomson is the latest Academy graduate to pledge his future to the club and he added that he is hopeful Brendan Rodgers’ background in youth football will continue to see players like him be given the chance to shine at first-team level.“The manager started out as a youth coach and worked his way up so he’s someone who knows what it’s like to work with younger players and he’ll give all of us good advice, feedback and tell us what we need to make it.“Hopefully he’ll give myself and a few other lads a chance.“I’m very thankful to Ronny Deila for giving me the chance to make not only my competitive debut but for giving me a chance in the pre-season friendlies but there is a new manager now and it's a new start so I have to do well to try to impress him.“I just need to progress as a player and if I can work on the aspects that I know I need to improve on then hopefully I can make a real push. If not, maybe I need to go out and play some form of first-team football but that’s hopefully going to be here.”
