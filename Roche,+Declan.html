Celtic Career Review

Signed from St. Kevin's Boy's Club aged 16 and played youth and reserve team football until he joined Partick Thistle on a free transfer in January 1991. Later returned to Ireland and turned out for Cork, Shelbourne and Derry before a spell on the Irish junior circuit with Portmarnock. He represented Ireland at Junior level in their victorious 'Quadrangular Tournament' campaign in April 1998 in addition to his underage caps gained at senior level.

He had fallen out with Partick Thistle manager John Lambie who hit him in the face with a dead or dying pigeon during the course of a dressing room bust-up. Chic Charnley's account in Seeing Red: The Chic Charnley Story (2009) adds that Lambie's bizarre assault was accompanied with the words: "See you, you Irish b*****d, you're as useful as this doo".* Charming!
As of 2018 Roche is himself a coach, of the soccer team at Dublin City University (DCU).
*Doo is a Scottish colloquialism for pigeon.
