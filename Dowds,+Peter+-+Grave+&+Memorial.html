Dowds, Peter - Grave & Memorial

Celtic Graves | Player Biog

Mar 2014

A remembrance even was held by the Celtic Graves Society, who marked the day with a headstone to replace the blank space to give Peter the respect he deserves.
Photographs (c) Patrick McGuire photography.




Pre-2014
Peter Dowds lies in an unmarked grave in Abbey Cemetery, Elderslie.

"To the present generation Peter Dowds is not even a name, but to old-timers he was the greatest-ever, at home in any and every position, the equal of a Doyle or Kelly in defence, of Madden on the right, Campbell on the left, Cassidy at centre . . . " - Willie Maley

His grave is only a short distance away from fellow Johnstone Celt, Peter Scarff.

An unbelievable site. Well done to all concerned.

My main purpose in writing is to ask if it's o.k. to update some of the records on the Johnstone Celts as I have factual data to share:

I have checked civil records for PETER DOWDS birth and can confirm he was born in Johnstone - under surname Douds.

There is fascinating info available on Celts born in the old Abbey Parish (Parts of Paisley; Elderslie Johnstone, Kilbarchan and Linwood..possibly Howood and Houston)

I came across a photo of Peter Dowds in a book of Johsntone's history: he was playing for a team called Johnstone Harp in 1887 -other links to Celtic in that photo may include one of our earliest keepers W Dunning and the trainer Thompson (ex powderhall sprint champion) who may be the father or uncle of another Johnstone Bhoy the famous Bertie Thompson who played and scored a few goals in the cup winning teams in 1930's. A team-mate of John Thompson and Peter Scarff who both died tragically whilst still Celtic players.

My great, great grandfather played in the 1887 Johnstone Harp team with Dowds and Dunning.

I am also a distant relation to Peter Scarff who was another Abbey Celt -although I am fairly sure he was from Linwood not Johnstone- will check.

The very first Scottish Cup winning team 1892 (replay) was captained by Johnstone Bhoy Patrick Gallagher (the actual spelling may be Gallacher) and his team-mate Peter Dowds was also from the toun. I can only imagine at the celebrations in the local bars that night (there was a Celtic Bar in the town at that time and might have been a wee bit busy that night...:-)

Also another Patrick Gallagher (again sp. may be Gallacher) played a few games and scored against Hearts in the early teams

I suspect that Dan Doyle from Paisley end of the old Abbey Parish, was connected to these guys

Can I update records and add new info?

Yes I know the graveyard well- my grandparents are there plus many extended family

Peter Douds (Dowds) was born in Johnstone in 1871 to John Douds and Susan Mc Ruddden (var sp. McReading) they were married at St Fillan's in Houston in 1854 as the chapel of St Margaret's in Johnstone was not consecrated until slightly later.

I will check into the death details later to confirm details and pass on any info I find then.

Hoping to do this as a short autobio - with some refers. to other local Celts because I supsect there was a network of football playing families connecting together in the area.

The Johnstone Harp team of 1887 -I mentioned above had Kelly; Dunnion (Dinnning?); Thomson and two Dowds M and Peter whom I believe were linked to the earliest Celtic teams.

The foto from 1887 shows the Harp team in a hooped strip (I am guessing it must be green and white) Peter Dowds is lying on the foreground in the manner of the time -reserved for the star player

When Celtic won the cup in 1892 -there must have been a huge celebration in Johnstone as captain/two first team players born/lived there-perhaps local press cuttings from the period will reveal something interesting?

I did discover that one of the 1888-1893 era Patrick Gallachers (x2 spellings born in Houston area lived in Johnstone ) died in Kelvin district of Glasgow -will share anything I find.
