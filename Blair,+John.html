Biog

John Blair joined the Bhoys from the famous Belfast Celtic in 1910 but his stay at Glasgow's Celtic Park was to prove short lived. He had come to the shipyards at Clydebank and Celtic then took him on.

He was an international player who had previously won caps as an amateur for Ireland in 1907 (v Wales) and 1908 (v Scotland & England).

After making his debut appearance at Parkhead on 18th November 1910, in a 0-0 draw v Third Lanark, the forward was to never appear for the Celts first team again in a major fixture and was transferred to Motherwell at the end of the season.

It wasn't to be for John Blair at Celtic, but he was one of the fortunate number to have had the honour to have played for both Celtic & Belfast Celtic (two spells with the latter).

He was to spend most of his playing career in the Irish leagues, and after hanging up his boots, he was to later serve as President of the then Irish League in 1921/22 in what was a period of major unheavel in the Emerald Isle.

He passed away in 1934.
