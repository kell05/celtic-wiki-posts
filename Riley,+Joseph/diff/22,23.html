Biog

Joseph Riley was a member of the Maryhill Hibernian side which lifted the Scottish Junior Cup in 1928.

The inside-forward was signed by the Bhoys soon after that victory and he made his debut on 11th August 1928 in a 1-0 league victory at Dens Park.

It had been hoped that the Bonnybridge-born player would be the man to replace the wayward genius of Tommy McInally. That was though quite a big ask for any player as McInally was a great talent and quite a spark on the field, and players like him have never been ten-a-penny. It's quite a burden of expectation or hope for any aspiring player.

Regardless of the aim offor him to replace Tommy McInally, he did give some some decent performances, including scoring twice in a 4-4 draw v Queen's Park in only his second game in Oct 1928. Jimmy McGrory got the other two goals that day, and it saved the FirstCeltic XIside from a defeat but sadly he couldn't repeat this feat and didn't score again for the first team.

He played his games mostly in the first half of the 1928-29 season, after which he was replaced by Willie Hughes.

Celtic lost in only two of the matches Joe Riley played in, 3-0 v Rangers and 3-0 v Partick Thistle which was to be his final Celtic first team match. So his record in the team was good but Celtic had already begun a slow decline, the club's domestic hegemony ended around a decade back. That season Rangers were rampant and won the title by a long way.

Having never quite established himself at Parkhead, the Glasgow University student was to be freed in the summer of 1930, and he later made his way in time to St Mirren.

Joe Riley later went on to be a teacher.
