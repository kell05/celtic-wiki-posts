Biog

The versatile Alex King joined Celtic in May 1896 and his adaptability was to prove to be a valuable asset to the Bhoys.

A Scotland international King had come to the attention of the Celtic directors when he scored a hat-trick for Hearts in September 1895. So his capture was to be a real boone for the development of the club.

A coalminer by trade in Shotts,Shotts (Lanarkshire), King was snapped up by the Parkhead club who deployed the inside-left in a whole host of positions including goalkeeper (whenever the main goalkeeper was out for some reason).

He was quite a steal, as he had made his name in part by scoring a goal in the 3-1 Scottish Cup final victory for Hearts against Hibs (the only Scottish Cup final yet played outside of Glasgow, this one in Edinburgh). This time weCeltic wanted success for him in Glasgow.

His debut came in a 3-1 league defeat at Hibs on 15th August 15th,1896, butand then he scored in the next match a 7-2 league win over ClydeClyde. andHe made a fast name for himself scoring a double for the first team in a 6-0 win over Abercorn in only his fourth league game.game, heand so was to prove himself to be a solid and athletic performer for the next few seasons.

He was a near ever present in the First Team XI, and helped Celtic win the league title in 1897-98 and the Scottish Cup in 1899.

King played 62 times for Celtic in the league & Scottish Cup before a loss of form and confidence saw Celtic try to push his effort by cutting his wages. Not surprisingly it didn't work so he was sold to Dykehead in September 1900. However, he was out the side more than in it for most of the last season at Celtic, mostly deputising for Willie Orr it seems. So his lossdeparture was not as big a surprise as could have been.

As a Celt he was capped four times for Scotland, and six times in all, plus four times for the Scottish League select side.

He passed away in 1957.
