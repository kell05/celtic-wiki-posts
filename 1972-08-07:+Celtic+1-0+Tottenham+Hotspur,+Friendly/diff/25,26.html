Trivia

  • With the Drybrough Cup Final played at Hampden on the Saturday afternoon, this, the prestige pre-season friendly, was played on a Monday evening at Celtic Park with a 7:30pm kick off. Spurs were the holders of the UEFA Cup.
  • Tommy Callaghan and Kenny Dalglish were both carrying knocks from the Drybrough Cup Final. Callaghan made the game but Dalglish wasn't risked. Evan Williams was dropped in favour of Denis Connaghan. In came Jimmy Quinn at left back in place of Jim Brogan. Also in came Vic Davidson and Paul Wilson for Deans and Dalglish.


Review

There was no adventure or flair in this Spurs team for this game. A 0-0 draw would have suited them fine but Tommy Callaghan's fine low shot wins the day. Spurs played 4 across the middle with a back 4 and the game gradually descended into a midfield tussle.
Johnstone hit the bar and Knowles cleared a McNeill header off the line in the first half. In the second half Callaghan hit a fine goal from 20 yards out and Mcneill had another header cleared off the line.
Murdoch was Celtic's top man with a superb display of passing and positional play in midfield.

Callaghan long shot comes off

John Rafferty: Celtic 1, Spurs 0

Celtic beat Spurs in a match that was reminiscent of a European Cup-tie. Their problem was to pierce one of those well-ordered and heavily-manned defences that are the bane of the European cups. Winning to Jock Stein would be secondary to the sharpness which developed in such as Murdoch and Callaghan. Clearly the middle of the team is coming right and that must be satisfying. Connaghan in goal, although not heavily tried, looked safe, but as impressive as anybody was Quinn at left back. He tackled and covered safely and his tremendous speed was useful both in defence and in attack.

Spurs with this extravagantly placed collection of talent demonstrated the failing so long apparent in English foot­ball. They inhibited skill in their safe concentration on the midfield and defence. One kept thinking that if only they gave as much thought to scoring as to defence they would be a formidable team indeed but that applies to most English clubs.
