Biog

Centre-half Pat Donlevy played just one competitive game for Celtic after signing for the club from Glasgow Perthshire in March 1898, having previously played for Kilsyth Emmet and Duntocher Hibernian.

His solitary league appearance came on September 26th that year in a 2-1 defeat at home to Hibernian.

He actually played one other game for Celtic, against Rangers at Barrowfield for the "Engineers' Strike Fund".

He left Parkhead in October 1898 having never played another major competitive match for the first team again (he was part of a large scale cull of the reserves at that time), and made a move to Airdrie.

He had signed for Celtic in a league title winning season, but sadly Celtic never won the title the following season when he finally won a place for the first team.

He later moved to Thornliebank and Arthrulie.

He retired from playingplay in 1903.
