Jackson, John

J | Player Pics | World War One | A-Z of Players

Personal

Fullname: John Betram Jackson*
aka: John Jackson, Jock Jackson*
Born: 21 June 1893* [to be reviewed, could be 1890]
Died: 8 Aug 1971, Glasgow*
Birthplace: Dalry
Signed: 4 Jan 1909 (loan from Clyde); 30 Oct 1912 (loan from Clyde); 8 Sep 1917 (loan from Leeds City)
Left: May 1918 (signed up to Royal Scots Fusiliers); 31 Jan 1919 (to Clydebank)
Position: Utility
Debut: Celtic 0-3 Rangers, Glasgow Cup, 22 Sep 1917
Internationals: n/a
*please see CGS forum, as some of the above details (name, DoB etc) may need further investigation

Biog

John Jackson was a Dalry-born player who came to the Hoops on loan (three times), firstly from Clyde on 4 Jan 1909 & 30 Oct 1912 and then in September 1917 from Leeds City, although doesn't appear that he played in any games for Celtic in the main competitions in his first two loan spells but he was young in those first spells, and they were likely trials.

Aptly named, as being a 'utility player' he needed to be a 'jack of all trades'. He was also described as a 'brainy player' and a 'tremendous worker'.

"A heady, steady player, a useful man in attack at all times but at no time more useful than when a lead requires to be worked down."
"[John Jackson] possessed a wonderful amount of devil for his size and weight."

His debut came in a 3-0 home Glasgow Cup defeat to Rangers on 22 September 1917.

Prior to arriving at Celtic for his third stint, John was loaned to Rangers but never played a competitive game for the Ibrox side. So he was to amass an incredible three short loans to Celtic & Rangers, without any first team appearances. He finally won a match appearance in his third loan stint to Celtic. This was wartime and short-term loans were often needed to help meet needs as well as assisting players.

He was to be a mainstay in the Celtic side for most of the 1917/18 season, when Celtic relinquished the title after having dominated the domestic league during the difficult war years. Probably, his proudest moments were when he scored his two goals in two league matches, one in a 3-0 win over Queen's Park (Oct 1917) and then one in a 4-3 win over Motherwell (Dec 1917).

He was reportedly said to have had a stormer at right-half against Rangers in a Ne'er day derby in 1918, but on 2 Feb 1918 the whole of Hampden apparently rose in outrage over his none too gentle treatment of flying winger Alan Morton of Queen's Park.

He was the right-half again in the Celtic side that won the War Shield final versus Greenock Morton at Hampden (May 1918) and pocketed a Charity Cup medal v Partick Thistle three weeks later.

He once had to run all the way from Waverley Station to Easter Road on 17 Aug 1918 when his train was late and he could not get a taxi. Celtic still won the game 3-0 and Jackson still played in the game.

With the ability and enthusiasm to play in most outfield position he proved to be a useful signing for the Bhoys playing a total of 27 league games and scoring two goals before signing up in May 1918 to serve with the Royal Scots Fusiliers.

He did still play for Celtic for a handful of games at the start of the 1918/19 season, before McAvoy then Willie McStay took over Jackson's place in the side. Those games enabled him to be part of what was to be Celtic's league title winning squad of 1918/19 which marked the end of a golden era on the pitch for Celtic.

His last game for Celtic was in a Glasgow Cup final in Oct 1918 v Rangers which Celtic lost 2-0. It turned out to be practise for the battlefield, as John Jackson was to be laid out by Ranger's Tommy Cairns in the second half.

He moved onto Clydebank on loan in Jan 1919, before spells at Motherwell, Dundee and Stevenston Utd. He appears to have hung up his boots in 1922.

He passed away in 1971.*

From Leeds Utd/Leeds City History Site

Source: http://www.ozwhitelufc.net.au/leedscityprofiles.php/JacksonJB.php
Jackson: John Bertram (John)1913-1915 (Leeds City Player Details)
Inside ForwardBorn: Dalry, North Ayrshire: 21-06-1893
Debut: v Birmingham (a): 20-12-19135’6 1/2” 10st 2lb (1913)
Jackson commenced his career with Ardeer Thistle before joining Clyde in August 1908.

He went to Celtic on loan in January 1909 and later in October 1912.

Well known north of the border where he played for Clyde in the 1910 Scottish Cup Final against Dundee, which took two replays before Dundee won 2-1. Jackson put so much into the first replay that he collapsed during extra time. For five seasons until war began, Clyde were at the top end of Division One and reached the Scottish Cup final in 1909-10 and 1911-12.

The former of these finals was especially disappointing. For eighty-three minutes Clyde held a 2-0 lead with goals from Chalmers and Booth, and looked certain to win. With the Cup in sight, nerves got the better of Clyde and Robertson fluffed a clearance off Blair and into his own net. Dogged Dundee fought all the way and salvaged the game in the last minute with an equaliser from Langlands.

The replay was far a more cagey affair and ended goalless after extra time but with Dundee looking physically stronger. The third game was again a tight affair with Clyde scoring after only three minutes through Chalmers. Dundee equalised before the interval and with Clyde's energy sapped, John "Sailor" Hunter blasted Dundee to victory.

Jackson joined Leeds City in December 1913 for £1,000. He was an outstanding player at Leeds where he stayed until the war saw the Football League cease the fixtures at the end of the 1914-15 season. He did not quite live up to his large transfer fee but one of his best games came in the 4-2 F.A. Cup First Round victory over Gainsborough trinity, when he scored twice. He scored ten goals in fifty-four League games and another two in four F.A. Cup-ties.

He went on loan to Ayr United in December 1915, was then loaned to Rangers in May 1917, but never played for the first team, and Celtic in September 1917. He made his Celtic debut in a 3-0 home Glasgow Cup defeat to Rangers on 22nd September 1917. Prior to arriving at Celtic John was loaned to Rangers but never played a competitive game for the Ibrox side.

During the War he was reported to have run from Waverley Station in Edinburgh to the Hibernian’s ground in Easter Road to play in a game for Celtic because he could not catch a taxi, in his third loan spell with the Parkhead club.

With the ability and enthusiasm to play in most outfield position he proved to be a useful signing for the Hoops playing twenty-seven games and scoring two goals before leaving in May 1918 to serve with the Royal Scots Fusiliers.

He joined Clydebank on loan in January 1919, Motherwell in October 1919, Dundee in December 1919 and Stevenston United on loan in 1920. He played one representative game for the Scottish League. [ He played one representative game for the Scottish League, when he appeared at Inside Right for the League against the Irish League at Windsor Park, Belfast on 11th November 1912. ]
