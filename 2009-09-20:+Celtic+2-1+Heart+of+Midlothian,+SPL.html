2009-09-20: Celtic 2-1 Heart of Midlothian, SPL

Match Pictures | Matches: 2009 - 2010 | 2009-2010 Pictures

Trivia

  • Celtic go clear top for first time this season. 2pts in front, Rangers drew v Kilmarnock day before.
  • Robson on bench for first time in a long long time.
  • Samaras out injured, so Killen started with Skippy up front.

Review

Sometimes you just got to take the three points no matter the performance. There were few positives from this game, but when you come from one nil down and win at the death (a header by Loovens) you should more over-joyed than relieved. Celtic were down early to a goal by Hearts and we did little to give the support confidence that we would make up for it in the first half. A shot off the bar by Maloney was the only real one and we were hopeless in much of our defensive work lucky not to go two nil down (Caldwell clearing a chance off the line).

Second half was better and we came out fighting. Lots more goal mouth action from us (and even Hearts had chances) but we were dominant and we were deserving of the good work that got us the goal from Killen. A late injury time corner got us a goal from Loovens and the relief was immeasureable. We were now top of the league solely and we were happy. Lots still to work on.

Loovens goal relieves pressure on Mowbray: Celtic 2 Hearts 1 KIllen 56, Loovens 90 Santana 5

21 September 2009
Provided by: The Guardian

A rousing second half and a smattering of good fortune saved Tony Mowbray from his first crisis as Celtic's manager.
Mowbray had heard his players jeered off at the interval against Heart of Midlothian and Celtic's task in the second half would have been even greater had Artur Boruc not saved brilliantly from Andrew Driver. Hearts were a goal ahead by that point and an eight-day spell in which Celtic had dropped two points at home to Dundee United and lost to a distinctly mediocre Hapoel Tel-Aviv side in the Europa League was clearly proving too much for the fans.
It was also proving a problem for Boruc and his team-mate Gary Caldwell, who had a heated verbal spat. Salvation, amid controversy, was at hand. Chris Killen scored the equalising goal before Glenn Loovens sealed victory three minutes into injury time. The celebrations in the stands said all that needed to be known about how the three points were treasured.
"Expectancy is high at this club," said Mowbray. "Fans do not want to hear me saying how well we have played after losing a game 1-0. Hopefully they went home happy. I thought we played exceptionally well when we had the ball."
Hearts appeared to have a genuine grievance over the corner which led to that winning goal, the full-back Lee Wallace letting the ball run out of play in the belief the Celtic substitute Niall McGinn had taken the last touch. The mere fact that there were four minutes of stoppage time added was curious after a second half in which neither physio came on to the field and no player was spoken to for time-wasting.
The Hearts manager, Csaba Laszlo, had been a relieved man before the late drama. Wallace and McGinn had tangled in an incident which could easily have led to a Celtic penalty. On the other hand, Shaun Maloney appeared to foul Suso Santana before supplying the cross from which Killen headed home.
"The amount of added time is my biggest question," said Laszlo. "You must ask the referee about this. Lee Wallace felt 100% that it was not a corner and Suso was of the opinion that he was fouled."
While this was a welcome win for Celtic, who are two points clear of Rangers at the top of the league, Mowbray's troubles are a evident. A more potent team than Hearts would have punished Celtic's defensive chaos while the ongoing struggles of Scott Brown are apparent.
The Scotland midfielder may be an influential part of Celtic's team but he is short of fitness. Mowbray would have little cover if Brown had sustained treatment on his ankle, yet there will come a time when the manager must decide that the player is doing neither himself nor his club any favours by ploughing on.
For Hearts' goal, after five minutes, Santana cavorted unchallenged through the Celtic midfield before firing a shot below Boruc from 25 yards. Celtic, who had been utterly abject during that opening half, displayed renewed vigour after the break, prompted by Aiden McGeady. Hearts survived two scrambles in front of goal before Killen scored his first goal of the season and Loovens headed beyond Janos Balogh.
The defender Glenn Loovens scored an injury-time header to beat Hearts

Celtic manager Tony Mowbray:
"I thought we were very dominant in the first half.
"As a coach, you've always got to look past the result and look at the performance.
"Regardless of whether we lost one-nil or won two-one, I think the performance of the team was very good.
"We played a lot of good football, got into some fantastic areas, but the final third let us down at stages.
"We didn't ask enough questions, but to play to get there was outstanding."
Hearts manager Csaba Laszlo:
"You must ask what Suso and Andrew Driver what they were thinking allowing Maloney to pick up the ball for the first goal.
"Then the referee somehow found four minutes of stoppage time and, for the second goal, we had the possibility to keep the ball instead of giving away the corner.
"Lee Wallace says it was not a corner, but if we play the ball up front the referee has no choice but to blow the whistle to end the game and we have a point.
"I am not angry with my players, just disappointed. At these moments, we have to be more clever."
